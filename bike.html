Day Rides

Platte River Trail

Located just of the C-470 in Littleton, this is a scenic ride all along the South Platte river leading you to Confluence Park

The trail overall spans 30 miles with little to no elevation gain, making it possible for riders of all skill levels to complete. The path is paved the entire way, with clear signage. Stop for lunch at any of Denver's great restaurants and breweries or stop off at REI for a quick tune-up before hitting the trail again!

Cherry Creek Trail

The Cherry Creek Regional Trail is a picturesque 40-mile route that begins in downtown Denver and connects suburban and rural Arapahoe and Douglas Counties. The majority of the trail is paved, but short sections of the more rural southern end consist of gravel trail and road.

The trail begins at the Platte River Trail in Denver's Confluence Park and takes riders through Denver into various suburbs, eventually emerging in Cherry Creek State Park. The trail is largely flat with small elevation gains.

C-470 Trail

The C-470 Bikeway—also known as the C-470 Trail and Centennial Trail in Douglas County—provides open views of the Colorado foothills as it follows the southwestern segment of Denver's beltway. The trail provides access to multiple destinations such as Chatfield Lake, Green Mountain and Bear Creek Lake Park, as well as large shopping centers in Centennial like Park Meadows where you can find numerous dining and shopping options.

The paved trail connects to many other regional and local trails, including the High Line Canal Trail, Mary Carter Greenway (Arapahoe Greenway), Bear Creek Trail and Cherry Creek Regional Trail.

US 36 Bikeway

The U.S. 36 Bikeway is a 15-mile long paved pathway that follows alongside the Boulder Turnpike, U.S. 36, from the south end of Boulder to Westminster in Central Colorado. The trail connects to other local trails along the way as well as allowing connections to many other major roadways. The route goes past Superior, the Flatiron Crossing shopping complex and the Interlocken office complex.

The entire trail is largely paved and spans about 32 miles round trip. This is a great ride on the weekend or during the week commuting to/from work, or the after work ride.
